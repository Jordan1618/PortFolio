# **Within and its cousins*

Within means inside, or at the core of something - a space, a group, a framework, a limit. For example, An OU groups objects within the AD means inside the AD, within its scope. The difference from in is that in is neutral and general purpose, while within emphasizes the idea of a boundary or framework. Within the budget, within the company - it sounds more formal and structural than in.

### Cousins of within for place or belonging

|Word|Meaning|Example|
|---|---|---|
|within|inside, within the limits of something (formal, structural)|within the domain|
|inside|inside (physical, concrete)|inside the building|
|in|in (generic, all purpose)|in the AD|
|among|in the middle of a group of distinct elements|among the OUs|
|amid|in the middle of (literary, often abstract or chaotic context)|amid the crisis|
|throughout|covering the whole thing|throughout the domain|

Practical rule: for IT or admin technical writing, within sounds more professional and precise. In would also work but feels flatter, less like official documentation.
