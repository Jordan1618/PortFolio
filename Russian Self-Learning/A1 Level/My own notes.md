## 1) The Present Tense and Negation

There are several key rules to remember:

- **Zero Copula**: The verb "to be" is not used in the present tense (e.g., "I am Jordan" is simply "I Jordan").
    
- **Pronoun Usage**: A pronoun is used at all times for clarity.
    
- **Negation**: Negation is formed by placing **не** ("nyé") directly before the verb.
    
- **Verbal Aspect**: Verbs have two states: **Imperfective** and **Perfective**.
    
    - The present tense is always **Imperfective** because the action is ongoing.
        
    - The **Perfective** aspect designates a completed action or a result.
        

### Verb Conjugation Groups

Verbs are categorized by their endings:

1) The -ать / -ять / -еть Group

- **Я (I)**: -ю
    
- **Ты (You - sing.)**: -ешь
    
- **Он/Она/Оно (He/She/It)**: -ет
    
- **Мы (We)**: -ем
    
- **Вы (You - plur./formal)**: -ете
    
- **Они (They)**: -ют
    
- _Examples_: Знать (To know), Понимать (To understand), Работать (To work), Думать (To think), Делать (To do/make), Гулять (To walk/stroll), Уметь (To be able/know how), Иметь (To have).
    

2) The -ить Group

- **Я (I)**: -ю / -у
    
- **Ты (You - sing.)**: -ишь
    
- **Он/Она/Оно (He/She/It)**: -ит
    
- **Мы (We)**: -им
    
- **Вы (You - plur./formal)**: -ите
    
- **Они (They)**: -ят / -ат
    
- _Examples_: Говорить (To speak), Курить (To smoke), Любить (To love), Готовить (To prepare/cook).

## 2) Pronunciation and Phonetics

- **The letter Е**: Pronounced as "yé" at the beginning of a word or after a vowel. After a consonant, it is pronounced as "é".
    
- **The letter Ё**: Always pronounced as "yo".
    
- **Consonant + Hard Vowel (А, О, У, Э, Ы)**: Results in a "dry" or hard sound.
    
- **Consonant + Ь (Soft Sign) or Soft Vowel (Я, Ё, Ю, И, Е)**: The consonant becomes "moist" or palatalized (the "ghostly i" sound).
    
- **Consonant + Ъ (Hard Sign) + Vowel**: Marks a distinct break or pause between sounds.
    

### The "11 Rebels" (Irregular Verbs)

These verbs end like Group 1 but conjugate according to Group 2 rules:

- Смотреть (To watch/look)
    
- Видеть (To see)
    
- Ненавидеть (To hate)
    
- Терпеть (To endure/tolerate)
    
- Обидеть (To offend)
    
- Вертеть (To turn/twirl)
    
- Зависеть (To depend)
    
- Слышать (To hear)
    
- Дышать (To breathe)
    
- Держать (To hold)
    
- Гнать (To chase/drive)

## 3) The Nominative Case (Subject Case)

While French and English use syntax (word order) to define meaning, Russian uses **endings** (declensions). The Nominative is the "base" form of the word, used for the subject or an interjection.

**Gender Terminations**:

- **Masculine**: Ends in a hard consonant, -й, or -ь.
    
- **Feminine**: Ends in -а, -я, -ия, or -ь.
    
- **Neuter**: Ends in -о, -е, -ие, or -мя.
    

Examples:

| **Word (Cyrillic)** | **Gender** | **Translation** |
| ------------------- | ---------- | --------------- |
| Стол                | Masculine  | Table           |
| Музей               | Masculine  | Museum          |
| Словарь             | Masculine  | Dictionary      |
| Папа                | Masculine  | Dad             |
| Мама                | Feminine   | Mom             |
| Песня               | Feminine   | Song            |
| Россия              | Feminine   | Russia          |
| Ночь                | Feminine   | Night           |
| Окно                | Neuter     | Window          |
| Море                | Neuter     | Sea             |
| Здание              | Neuter     | Building        |
| Имя                 | Neuter     | Name            |

Note: Words like **Папа** (Dad), **Дедушка** (Grandpa), **Мужчина** (Man), and **Дядя** (Uncle) are masculine in meaning but follow feminine ending patterns.
