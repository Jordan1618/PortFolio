# Russian Self-Learning: Strategic Linguistic Expansion

## Purposes:
Having mastered English, I am now tackling a new alphabet for its mental stimulation and strategic value. My goal is to master the Russian language to access the depth of Slavic culture, its Russo-Mongol and Orthodox roots, and to understand the global influence of the Russophone academy and its future role in international geostrategy. Yeah, Geopolitics/Geostrategies are a main core of my understanding on the world

## Tech Stack & Learning Pillars:
* **Foundations:** Cyrillic alphabet (33 letters) and Akanie phonetics (stressed vowel logic).
* **Grammar & Conjugation:** Mastery of the case system and the two main verb groups (Type I & II) with all tenses.
* **Orthography:** Focus on Slavic script, including cursive and specific spelling rules.
* **Active Practice:** Using Gemini Live for real-time pronunciation audits and daily life conversation.

## Method:
I follow a rigorous self-study system based on five pillars: **Grammar, Conjugation, Orthography, Pronunciation, and Daily Usage**. I transform raw data into atomic Markdown summaries to master sentence logic and word stress. My progress is tracked via a "X Vocabulary words" challenge with an AI audit every 30 words on all previous words.
