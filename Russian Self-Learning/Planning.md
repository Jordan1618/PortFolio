### A1: Beginner — The Foundations

- **Cyrillic Alphabet:** 33 letters, basic reading, and cursive writing. 🇷🇺
    
- **Phonetics:** Hard/soft vowels, reduction of unstressed **О** into **А** (Akanie), and fixed word stress.
    
- **Nominative Case:** Subject of the sentence; genders (masculine, feminine, neuter).
    
- **Basic Pronouns:** я, ты, он/она/оно, мы, вы, они.
    
- **Present Imperfective:** 1st and 2nd conjugation groups (я, ты, он…); absence of the verb "to be".
    
- **Basic Interrogatives (Added):** Who (**Кто**), What (**Что**), Where (**Где** / **Куда**), Why (**Почему**), How (**Как**). 
    
- **Basic Negation:** **не** + verb; **нет** (no / there is not).
    

### A2: Elementary — Basic Structure

- **Genitive Case:** Possession, absence (**нет** + genitive), and quantity.
    
- **Dative Case:** Recipient and age (мне 20 лет).
    
- **Prepositional Case:** Location (в, на) and "talking about" (о).
    
- **Accusative Case:** Direct object (inanimate nouns only).
    
- **Verbs of Position (Added):**
    
    - **State:** **стоять** (stand), **лежать** (lie), **висить** (hang), **сидеть** (sit).
        
    - **Action:** **положить** (lay down), **поставить** (stand up/place). 
        
- **Basic Motion Verbs:** **идти** vs **ходить** (on foot); **ехать** vs **ездить** (by vehicle).
    

### B1: Intermediate — Consolidation

- **Instrumental Case:** Means/tool, profession, and company (с кем).
    
- **Plural in 6 Cases:** Complete endings for nouns and adjectives.
    
- **Aspectual Choice:** Process, repetition, or result.
    
- **Relative Pronouns (Added):** Using "**который**" (who/which) to link complex sentences. 🔗
    
- **Adverbs (Added):** Formation of adverbs from adjectives (ending in **-о**).
    
- **Extended Negation:** никто, ничто, никогда, нигде (mandatory double negation).
    

### B2: Upper Intermediate — Fluency

- **Advanced Motion Verbs:** Prefixed verbs (при-, у-, вы-, за-, до-, пере-) and figurative meanings.
    
- **Mobile Stress (Added):** Understanding how the accent shifts between singular and plural (e.g., **рука́** vs **ру́ки**), affecting pronunciation. 
    
- **Participles & Gerunds:** Active and passive forms; replacing subordinate clauses.
    
- **Punctuation (Added):** Mandatory commas before "**что**" or "**который**".

### C1: Advanced — Stylistic Precision

At this level, the focus is on **technical mastery** and the ability to adapt your speech to different social contexts.

- **Language Registers:** Learning to switch between colloquial (informal), neutral, and formal/literary styles.
    
- **Complex Syntax:** Mastering very long sentences with multiple subordinate clauses and participial constructions.
    
- **Derivational Morphology:** Understanding how prefixes (like **пере-** or **раз-**) and suffixes change the core meaning of a root word.
    
- **Rare Declensions:** Learning exceptions such as heteroclite nouns (**время**, **имя**) or indeclinable words (**кино**, **кофе**).
    

### C2: Proficiency — Total Mastery

This is the "Bilingual" level where the language becomes an **instinctive tool** for rhetoric and nuance.

- **Stylistic Aspect:** Using the imperfective for politeness or the perfective to mark abruptness/urgency.
    
- **Figurative Meanings:** Mastering metaphorical uses of motion verbs (e.g., "time flies" or "rumors are spreading").
    
- **Native Intuition:** The ultimate goal is to choose the correct aspect or case instinctively, without consciously thinking about the grammar rule.
    
- **Slang & Neologisms:** Integrating modern vocabulary and slang while maintaining correct grammatical morphology.
