## Grammatical Foundations (Level C2)

_Synthesized from the goal of full operational mastery_

### 1. Beyond Grammar — Into Style

- At C2, grammatical correctness is assumed. The work is entirely stylistic: choosing _which_ grammatically correct option carries the right register, connotation, and rhetorical effect.
- **Канцелярит** (bureaucratic clutter) is the main enemy of clean Russian prose — recognizing and eliminating it is a C2 editorial skill.

### 2. Phonological Completion

- **Mobile stress** is the last major accuracy barrier for non-native speakers. Errors in stress patterns (рука́ → ру́ки, not ру́ки → рукú) are immediately audible to native speakers and disappear only with systematic study and massive listening exposure.

### 3. Cultural Embedding

- **Intertextuality:** Russian educated speech is saturated with literary and biblical allusions. Recognizing a half-quoted Pushkin line or a Soviet-era slogan reframing in a newspaper headline requires cultural as much as linguistic knowledge.

## Study Log & Milestones (C2 Targets)

- **Precision:** Edit your own written Russian for канцелярит, redundancy, and register inconsistency.
- **Oral fluency:** Produce spontaneous speech with natural prosody, mobile stress, and register-appropriate colloquial reductions.
- **Cultural:** Read and analyze a major 20th-century Russian novel (Булгаков, Пастернак, Солженицын) without a dictionary.
- **Professional:** Draft legally and institutionally accurate Russian documents (contracts, strategic notes, diplomatic correspondence).
